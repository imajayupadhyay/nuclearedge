About Karol Bagh

Karol Bagh is a locality situated in the Central District of Delhi, India. It is a diverse area that combines residential and commercial spaces and is renowned for its shopping streets like Ghaffar Market and Ajmal Khan Road. Previously, it was the constituency for the Karol Bagh Lok Sabha until its discontinuation in 2008.

INSIGHTS ABOUT Karol Bagh

Highlights about this area

Karol Bagh enjoys a central location in Delhi, making it easily accessible from various parts of the city
Karol Bagh is well-liked among the affluent residents of West and Central Delhi for its shopping options, featuring bustling markets like Ajmal Khan Road and Arya Samaj Road
Renowned as one of Delhi's oldest shopping hubs, Karol Bagh market boasts a diverse range of establishments, from large air-conditioned showrooms to quaint shops tucked away in narrow alleys.
Delhi Sarai Rohilla and Delhi Kishanganj are the closest railway stations to Central Karol Bagh, both approximately one kilometer away
It is accessible via the Karol Bagh Metro station, part of the Blue Line of the Delhi Metro.
Karol Bagh is situated around 20 to 22 kilometers from the Indira Gandhi International Airport and 4.1 kilometers from New Delhi Railway Station

What stands out as positive in this area?

With good connectivity through public transport and major roads, residents can commute conveniently within and outside the locality
The area provides access to essential amenities such as schools, hospitals, banks, and recreational facilities
Proximity to commercial areas and markets creates job opportunities and business prospects for residents
Karol Bagh retains its historical charm with old buildings, temples, and landmarks, adding character to the locality

What stands out as negative in this area?

The dense population and commercial activities can result in overcrowded streets and limited open spaces
